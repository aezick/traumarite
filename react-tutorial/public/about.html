Trauma Rite
  • About
trauma·rite (n,v)

  1. a social and psychological, internal and external ritual of marking and scrubbing the survivor body post-assault

  2. a performance of trauma

Trauma Rite is a performance piece publically displayed at the intersection of North University and State St., Ann Arbor, MI. It reflects personal experiences of survivorship, trauma, and vulnerability. It seeks to trouble the disembodiment of survivors post-assault and the discursive erasure of individual pain in the diagnosis of PTSD.

This piece is by no means attempting to define survivors’ experiences at large. Every act of sexual violence is an announcement of power onto an individual - an individual whose interiority is as continuously valid and diverse as their survival is perpetually beautiful.

Eliza Karin Cadoux is a gender studies student, multimedia artist, and feminist shitkicker currently residing of Ann Arbor, MI. They are a member of the performance group Call Your Mom, whose most recent show, This Close, covered themes of miscommunication and mistranslation through audio and video art, movement and built environment. They are the co-author, alongside writer Elle Gover, of Four / Fifteen, a poetry collection surrounding loss and solidarity. Trauma Rite is their first solo performance work.
